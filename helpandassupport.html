Frequently Asked Questions

How do I report road damage?

To report road damage, click on the "Report Damage" button on the main screen. Select the damage type, severity, add a description, and include a photo if possible. Your report will be saved and shared with relevant authorities.

How accurate is the AI damage detection?

Our AI model has been trained on thousands of road damage images and has an accuracy of approximately 92% for common damage types like potholes and cracks. However, it's always good to verify the AI's assessment before submitting a report.

Can I use the app without an internet connection?

Yes, you can capture damage reports offline. The app will store your reports locally and automatically sync them when you're back online. Note that AI analysis requires an internet connection.

How is my location data used?

Your location data is only used to pinpoint the exact location of road damage for reporting purposes. We never share your personal location data with third parties. You can disable location tracking at any time in settings.

How do I update my profile information?

To update your profile, go to the main menu and select "My Profile". From there you can edit your personal information, change your profile picture, and update your notification preferences.

Quick Start Guide

Getting Started with Road Damage Detection

1. Setting Up Your Account: After installing the app, create your account with your email address. You'll be asked to provide basic information and set your notification preferences.

2. Reporting Damage: Use the "Report Damage" button to document road issues. Take clear photos, select the appropriate damage type, and add a description for context.

3. Using the AI Scanner: The AI damage detection can help identify and classify road damage. Point your camera at the damage and let the AI suggest the damage type and severity.

4. Viewing Reports: Access your submitted reports from the main menu to see status updates and any actions taken by authorities.

Tips for Best Results

Photo Guidelines: Take photos in good lighting conditions. Get close enough to show details but include some context of the surrounding area. If safe, include an object for scale (like a coin or pen).

Accuracy Tips: Enable GPS for precise location tracking. Use the AI analysis as a guide but verify its suggestions before submitting. Provide clear descriptions including approximate dimensions when possible.

Safety First: Never put yourself in danger to document road damage. If you're in a vehicle, pull over safely before using the app. Be aware of your surroundings when taking photos.
